# CSE Base Rates — verified fact-sheet

*Source of truth for the web page. Every figure carries a category label, a year, a denominator/definition, and a real resolving URL. Where a number is uncertain or unverified-in-this-pass, it says so. Neutral by construction: it carries findings that cut in opposite directions and refuses to let either erase the other.*

---

## The one distinction this whole page rests on

Three different things are constantly conflated. **A figure for one is not a figure for another.** Most public argument about "grooming gangs" runs on quietly swapping one for another.

| | Category | What it covers | Rough scale, England & Wales |
|---|---|---|---|
| **(a)** | **CSA** — child sexual **abuse** | *All* sexual abuse of under-18s: intra-familial, online, lone opportunistic, institutional, peer-on-peer — and CSE within it | ~**500,000** children/year experience sexual abuse of *any* kind (prevalence estimate, not police count) |
| **(b)** | **CSE** — child sexual **exploitation** | The subset of CSA involving exchange/coercion (status, gifts, drugs, money). In police data it is a **flag** on a CSA offence, not a standalone offence | ~**17,100** CSE-flagged contact crimes, year to March 2024 (~15% of contact CSA) |
| **(c)** | **Group-based / "grooming-gang" CSE** | The subset of CSE with **two or more connected offenders** grooming and exploiting children | ~**700** recorded group-based-CSE offences, 2023 — *well under 1%* of recorded CSA. (Manual snapshot; the data-owners call it an undercount.) |

These are nested: **(c) ⊂ (b) ⊂ (a)**. The figures sit at opposite ends of the same funnel. Quoting an ethnicity finding about (c) as though it answered (a) — or vice versa — is the core category error this page exists to prevent.

- 500,000/year and 7%-report-to-police: Baroness Casey, *National Audit on Group-based Child Sexual Exploitation and Abuse*, June 2025, p.6 / p.~12 — https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/media/685559d05225e4ed0bf3ce54/National_Audit_on_Group-based_Child_Sexual_Exploitation_and_Abuse.pdf (prevalence originates with the CSA Centre)
- 100,000 total / 17,100 CSE-flagged / ~700 group-based CSE: Casey 2025, same PDF, pp.6, 56–58
- CSA Centre prevalence: https://www.csacentre.org.uk/research-resources/research-evidence/scale-nature-of-abuse/

**Universal caveat, applies to everything below:** CSA is a substantially hidden crime. The CSA Centre estimates ~1 in 10 children (≈15% of girls, ≈5% of boys) experience sexual abuse before 16; Casey notes that for "the vast majority… their abuse is not identified" and only ~**7%** of victims tell police at the time. **Every proportion here describes recorded / reported / surveyed cases — not all abuse.**

## 1. The headline claim and its provenance — the "~85% white" figure

**What the figure actually is:** a description of **(a) CSA — all forms** — specifically **prosecuted defendants whose ethnicity was recorded**. It is real and reasonably solid *for that category*. It is **not** a measured figure for group-based CSE, and the people who produced it say so.

### The most solid version (court data)
CSA Centre, *Child sexual abuse in 2023/24: Trends in official data* (March 2025), citing Ministry of Justice 2023 court data, England & Wales:

> "The ethnic background of defendants prosecuted for child sexual abuse offences was recorded in **69% (6,356) of cases** in 2023. Among these individuals, **nine-tenths were White (5,731)**… This is likely to be related to the **under-identification** of child sexual abuse in minority ethnic communities."

| Ethnicity | CSA defendants 2023 (of recorded only) | E&W population 10+ (2021 Census) |
|---|---|---|
| White (total) | **90%** | 83% |
| — White British | 87% | 75% |
| Asian (total) | 5% | 9% |
| — Pakistani | 2% | 2% |
| Black (total) | 2% | 4% |
| Mixed | 2% | 2% |

- Source: https://www.csacentre.org.uk/app/uploads/2025/03/Child-sexual-abuse-in-2023-24-Trends-in-official-data.pdf
- **DENOMINATOR FLAG:** "90% White" excludes the **31% of cases where ethnicity was not recorded**. It describes ~6,356 recorded defendants, not all ~9,215 CSA defendants that year.
- **VERIFICATION FLAG:** this PDF is image-based and did not text-extract in our pass. The 90%/69%/31% headline is internally consistent and matches the CSA Centre's public framing; treat the sub-rows (87% White British, 2% Pakistani, etc.) as **primary-but-not-independently-re-rendered**.

### Where the rounder "~85–88% white" number comes from (CPS FOI)
Full Fact, documenting CPS data, child-abuse-flagged sex-offence cases 2015/16 (~6,200 defendants): **White 67%, Asian 4%, Black 3%, Mixed 1%, Other 1%, not recorded 24%.** Of the **76% where ethnicity was recorded**, ~**88%** were White (67/76). The "~85–88%" only appears once the 24% unrecorded are stripped from the denominator.
- https://fullfact.org/crime/what-do-we-know-about-ethnicity-people-involved-sexual-offences-against-children/

### Earlier / corroborating CSA-wide figures
- CSA Centre, *Scale and nature* (June 2021): defendants "**from White ethnic backgrounds (92%) in 2019/20**." https://www.csacentre.org.uk/research-resources/scale-and-nature/ — **(year-specific, recorded-only; also image-PDF, treat as primary-but-unverified-in-pass).**
- Corroboration that *did* render — CSA Centre key-messages (2017 MoJ data): of recorded-ethnicity CSA defendants, **11% were BAME** vs **14% BAME** in the 2011 Census → **no over-representation in CSA generally.** Same conclusion, different year.

### Reliability verdict on the headline
- **Solid** as a description of **identified-and-prosecuted CSA defendants whose ethnicity was recorded** (~90% White 2023 / 92% 2019/20 / ~88% of recorded CPS 2015/16).
- **Caveat 1 — denominator:** describes only the 69–76% with ethnicity recorded; 24–31% unknown.
- **Caveat 2 — under-identification:** the CSA Centre attributes part of the White majority to under-reporting of CSA in minority-ethnic communities. It reflects **who is caught and prosecuted, not necessarily who offends.**
- **Caveat 3 — mis-application:** it is **NOT a reliable figure for group-based CSE (c).** Transferring it there is the error §3–§5 exist to stop.

### Early-census caveats — flag these on any chart
- **1981 — NO DATA EXISTS.** The 1981 UK Census had **no ethnic-group question** (dropped after the contested 1979 Haringey test). Pre-1991 minority numbers were *estimated* from country-of-birth and sample surveys, not counted. **Do not display a "1981 White %" as if measured.** https://hansard.parliament.uk/Commons/1980-04-02/debates/9fad160a-e47f-4433-8b7d-1eb071d473ae/1981Census(EthnicOrigin)
- **1991 — two caveats.** (1) **Geography:** 94.5% is **Great Britain**, not E&W. ONS-derived splits put **England 93.8% White, Wales 98.5%** in 1991 — so E&W-only ≈ **~94%** (England drags it just *below* GB; the direction holds, the value barely moves). (2) **No "White British" sub-category existed** — single "White" box until 2001. So for 1981/1991, only **White total** is meaningful. 1991 source = 1991 Census Local Base Statistics (Crown Copyright), re-hosted academically; **not located on a live ONS URL** — medium-high confidence on the number, with the geography caveat: https://dwowen.warwick.ac.uk/tab2.htm

### Younger cohorts are markedly less White British — use these for any young-offender comparison
Offending and victimisation concentrate in younger age bands, which are systematically less White British than the population *stock*.

- **2021 Census (ONS):** the White group has the **oldest** age structure (median age 43, vs 19 for Mixed/Multiple, 40 overall). https://www.ons.gov.uk/peoplepopulationandcommunity/culturalidentity/ethnicity/articles/ethnicgroupbyageandsexenglandandwales/census2021
- **DfE School Census, England state schools, Jan 2025 (2024/25):** **38.0%** of pupils are minority ethnic — DfE defines this as "**any origin other than White British**." https://explore-education-statistics.service.gov.uk/find-statistics/school-pupils-and-their-characteristics/2024-25

**CORRECTION — do not say "White British ≈ 62% of pupils."** 100% − 38.0% = 62%, but that residual is the *not-minority-ethnic* category, which **includes White-non-British pupils** (White Irish, White Other, Gypsy/Roma) and sits before unclassified pupils are removed. The **actual reported White British figure for 2024/25 is ≈ 60.3%** (the DfE release highlights only the 38.0% minority-ethnic headline; the 60.3% cell sits in the ethnicity data table). Trend is robust and corroborated: **64.9% White British in 2020/21 → ~60.3% in 2024/25** (the 2020/21 cell rendered live; the exact 2024/25 60.3% cell did **not** re-render in our pass — flag as primary-but-unverified-in-pass, direction firm).
- 2020/21 verified cell: https://explore-education-statistics.service.gov.uk/data-tables/permalink/dbdaa603-71e2-4050-8605-690fd0df4491

## 3. Who abuses children — relationship base rates, and how rare group-based CSE is

### Most CSA is by someone the child knows; stranger abuse is the minority
- **NSPCC / Radford et al. 2011** (6,196 interviews, UK): **>90%** of children who experienced *contact* sexual abuse were abused by **someone they knew** (~1 in 10 by a stranger). https://learning.nspcc.org.uk/research-resources/statistics-briefings/child-sexual-abuse
- **Crime Survey for England & Wales, y/e March 2019 (ONS)** — adults abused before 16 (multi-select, so >100%): **friend/acquaintance 37%, stranger 30%, family member ~a quarter to a third** (rising to ~a third–half where penetration involved); specific family: father 5%, stepfather 6%, mother 1%, other family member 22%. https://www.ons.gov.uk/peoplepopulationandcommunity/crimeandjustice/articles/childsexualabuseinenglandandwales/yearendingmarch2019
  - **Why CSEW's 30% stranger > Radford's ~10%:** different methods (retrospective adult recall vs. young-person survey), overlapping multi-select categories, different "stranger" definitions. **Not directly comparable** — but both show **known perpetrators dominate.** No clean three-way split summing to 100% exists; do not invent one.

### Intra-familial share
- **National Police Chiefs' Council (2023):** "abuse in the family environment accounts for **almost half** of all child sexual abuse offences reported to the police in England and Wales." Cleanest single intra-familial figure: **~50% of recorded CSA is within the family environment.** Within that, **~a quarter of cases involve a perpetrator under 18** (Children's Commissioner 2015). https://www.csacentre.org.uk/app/uploads/2023/09/Key-messages-from-research-on-intra-familial-child-sexual-abuse-2nd-edition.pdf

### The online shift
Online offending is now **~40% of recorded sexual-abuse crime**; of ~100,000 CSA/CSE offences in 2024, **~60% contact, ~40% online** (Casey 2025, p.6, p.55). Growth of online and child-on-child offending has pushed the average recorded offender age down (VKPP 2023: average CSAE offender age **24**; in COCAD 2023, **57% of suspects were under 18**).

### How rare group-based CSE is — the funnel, in numbers
First national estimate, police COCAD dataset (CSE Taskforce / Hydrant), via Casey 2025 (pp.6, 56–58):
- All CSA/CSE offences recorded 2024: **just over 100,000** (~60% contact, ~40% online).
- CSE flag (any CSE): **~17,100** crimes, y/e March 2024 (~15% of contact CSA).
- **Group-based contact CSA, 2023: 4,228 offences** — of which only **719 (17%) classified as CSE**. The rest: **familial 26%, institutional 9%, "other" 39% (largely child-on-child), unknown 9%.**
- **Group-based CSE specifically: ~700 recorded offences in 2023 — well under 1% of recorded CSA.**

**CRITICAL caveats on the ~700:** it is a **manually identified quarterly snapshot** across 43 forces with inconsistent systems → Casey: "**highly unlikely that this accurately reflects the true scale**" and "**may be an undercount**." IICSA earlier found "**over 70 live investigations** involving group-based CSE across England, Wales and Scotland," returns incomplete. **The true number is higher than ~700.** And do **not** quote the 4,228 / ~4% figure as "group-based CSE" — that is *all* group-based contact CSA (only 17% of it CSE).

### What makes group-based CSE criminologically distinctive
(Home Office 2020; Casey 2025) — it differs from the much larger intra-familial and online volumes on several axes:
1. **Multiple connected offenders** ("disinhibiting effect" of the group) — vs. overwhelmingly single-perpetrator familial abuse.
2. **Victims targeted by relative strangers in a shared local area** — the *opposite* of the dominant trusted-perpetrator CSA pattern; victims are often the most vulnerable (children in care, those with disabilities).
3. **Distinctive MO** — "boyfriend" model, parties/drugs/alcohol to lower resistance, passing victims between men, coercion.
4. **Peer recruitment** — victims coerced into introducing peers; self-propagating.
5. **Organisation / commercial element** — hotels, anonymous check-in; warrants major-crime tactics.
6. **Demographically homogeneous groups** — "broadly homogenous in age, ethnic background and socioeconomic status," with misogyny/"othering" enabling perpetrators to disregard victims.

### ETHNICITY FLAG across §3
Ethnicity is poorly recorded for CSA generally — national police data records perpetrator ethnicity for only ~**31% (VKPP)** to ~**34% (COCAD)** of suspects. **So none of the relationship base rates above carry a reliable ethnicity breakdown.** The largest CSA volumes (intra-familial ~50%, online ~40%) have **worse or absent** ethnicity recording than group-based CSE — meaning **no ethnicity comparison *between abuse types* is possible on national data.**

## 4. The base-rate principle + the Pakistan mirror

### The principle, stated crisply
> In any country the majority population commits the majority of most crimes — **automatically, by composition.** "Most offenders are [majority group]" is therefore **uninformative on its own**: it is a fact about the **denominator** (who lives there), not about the numerator's behaviour. The only statement carrying propensity information is a **per-capita rate**, compared against the group's **base rate** (its population share). Until you divide by the denominator, you have said nothing about whether any group offends more or less than expected.

With ~82% of England & Wales White (2021), a White plurality of offenders in almost any common offence is near-automatic — **before** propensity enters at all. Conversely, a group can be a **small share of all offenders** (small count, small denominator) yet still be **over-represented** in a specific subset (rate above its population share). **Both can be true at once.**

### The Pakistan mirror (illustrative logic, not an empirical claim about Pakistan)
Run the *same sentence* in a near-homogeneous country and its emptiness becomes obvious.

- Pakistan's **international migrant stock ≈ 3.26 million in 2019 ≈ 1.52% of population** (UN DESA / IOM), overwhelmingly **Afghan refugees** (UNHCR: ~1.3–1.5m registered). So **~98–99% of people in Pakistan are Pakistani nationals.**
- It follows by **arithmetic alone** that **~98–99% of *any* category of offender in Pakistan is Pakistani** — child abusers included. That sentence carries **zero** propensity information.
- "Most UK CSE offenders are white British" is **structurally the same sentence** — largely the ~74–82% White population reappearing in the offender column. It neither proves nor disproves over-representation of any minority. **Only a rate does that.**

What *is* known about Pakistani CSA comes mainly from **Sahil**, an Islamabad NGO whose *Cruel Numbers* compiles **press-reported** cases (a floor, **not** a prevalence rate): e.g. **2023 — 4,213 cases (~11 children/day), 53% girls / 47% boys, perpetrators most often acquaintances/relatives** — mirroring the UK finding that most CSA is by someone known, not gangs. **FLAG: Pakistan keeps no perpetrator-ethnicity statistics and has little reason to — "nationality of offender" there is as uninformative as "ethnicity of offender" is in any ~99%-one-group setting.**

- Sahil *Cruel Numbers*: https://sahil.org/cruel-numbers/ · Dawn report (2023): https://www.dawn.com/news/1818148
- UN DESA *International Migrant Stock 2020*: https://www.un.org/development/desa/pd/sites/www.un.org.development.desa.pd/files/files/documents/2021/Jan/undesa_pd_2020_international_migrant_stock_documentation.pdf
- World Bank migrant stock (% pop), Pakistan: https://data.worldbank.org/indicator/SM.POP.TOTL.ZS?locations=PK
- UNHCR Pakistan (Afghan refugees): https://www.unhcr.org/us/where-we-work/countries/pakistan
- **UNCERTAIN:** the exact per-year World Bank % decimals did not render to our tools; the ~1.5–2% band (Afghan-dominated) is robust across UN DESA / IOM / World Bank, anchored on UN/IOM **1.52% (2019)**. No precise single-year decimal beyond that is independently verified — stated rather than guessed.

### The mirror's twin on this site
The same axis from the opposite end: **Denmark** is the rare country that publishes the ancestry × crime cross-tab *and* an age- and socio-economically **standardised** criminality index — it does the per-capita division raw counts hide. Pakistan makes raw counts self-evidently meaningless by homogeneity; Denmark makes rate differences visible by standardising. Same principle, two extremes.
- Statistics Denmark standardised criminality index (tables STRAFNA3–9 / STRFNA10–14): https://www.dst.dk/en/Statistik/emner/sociale-forhold/kriminalitet/doemte-personer

## 5. The honest synthesis — two findings, two phenomena, both true

This is the load-bearing section, and it must hold **both** halves without slanting.

**Half one — the aggregate is largely base-rate-explained.** Across **all CSA (a)**, "most offenders are white" is true *and uninformative on its own*: it is mostly the ~74–82% White population reappearing in the offender column, the same structural fact as "~99% of Pakistan's abusers are Pakistani." The CSA Centre's 2017 cross-check (11% BAME defendants vs 14% Census BAME → no over-representation) confirms CSA-in-general tracks the population. Casey's own one-line summary of the aggregate:

> "If you look at the data for child abuse, it is not disproportionate, it is white men."

**Half two — AND THIS IS EXACTLY WHY the group-based subset is a separate, genuine signal.** Precisely *because* the aggregate is base-rate-explained, the aggregate cannot answer the question about **group-based CSE (c)**. Two official bodies reach the *same* two-part conclusion:

- **Home Office (2020), para 81 — an inference, not a measurement:** with the group-based data unusable, it judged it "most likely that the ethnicity of group-based CSE offenders is **in line with CSA more generally and with the general population, with the majority of offenders being White**" — while simultaneously recording (para 17, key-findings) that "a number of studies have indicated an **over-representation of Asian and Black offenders in group-based CSE**," that high-profile cases (Rotherham, Rochdale, Telford) "have mainly involved men of Pakistani ethnicity," and that "it is likely **no one community or culture is uniquely predisposed to offending.**"
- **Casey (2025) — the cleanest articulation of the (a)-vs-(c) split:**
  > "If you look at the data on child sexual exploitation, suspects and offenders, it is disproportionately Asian heritage. If you look at the data for child abuse, it is not disproportionate, it is white men."

**The two findings are about two different phenomena, and both are true:** the *aggregate* (a) is base-rate-explained; the *subset* (c) shows real over-representation in the localities where it can be measured. Neither cancels the other.

### Where the subset signal is real (local data, survives base-rate correction)
Casey found, in **three forces with better recording** (Greater Manchester, South Yorkshire, West Yorkshire), "clear evidence of over-representation among suspects of Asian and Pakistani-heritage men." The cleanest worked example — **Greater Manchester Police**:
- **61 multi-victim/multi-offender CSE investigations → 269 known suspects → 52% Asian** (141/269), 38% White (101/269).
- **35 strictly group-based CSE investigations → 243 known suspects → 54% Asian** (131/243), 35% White (85/243).
- vs **20.9% Asian** in Greater Manchester's 2021 Census population → "**more than twice as likely** to be of Asian ethnicity."
- *(Note the two distinct denominators: 52% is of the 269-suspect set; 54% is of the separate 243-suspect set. Do not blend them.)*
- **Victims** in the 35 group-based GMP investigations: **298 of 317 White (~94%)** (250 female + 48 male).
- **The same GMP force, across *all* CSA crimes** (781 cases, 908 suspects): suspect profile **much closer to the local population** (44% White, 16% unknown, 7% Asian) — i.e. the disproportion is **specific to the group-based subset**, not GMP's CSA in general. This single force shows both halves of the synthesis at once.
- Casey 2025, pp.74–82.

### Why "most are white" is contested *for grooming gangs specifically* — the missing-data fact
National group-based data is too incomplete to support *any* national ethnicity claim in either direction:
- **VKPP 2023:** ethnicity recorded for only **31%** of perpetrators; of those, **88% White, 5% Asian, 3% Black, 2% Mixed, 1% other**.
- **COCAD (Nov 2024):** self-defined ethnicity for only **34%** of suspects; of those, **88% White, 7% Asian, 5% Black, 3% Mixed, 2% Other** — Census-aligned. **BUT** COCAD covers *all* multi-perpetrator CSA (familial + institutional + child-on-child), which "obscures" grooming-gang CSE specifically.
- **Casey's reconfiguration — the single most important honesty number in the bundle:** put the ~two-thirds **unknown** back into the denominator and the published COCAD figures invert — only **28% of group-based perpetrators are *recorded* as White** (1,884 of 6,670; 66% "not declared"), and **39% of victims**. The "88% White" / "85–87% White victim" headlines are **denominator artefacts** of recording only a third of cases.
- Casey, verbatim: "it is **not possible to draw any conclusions on the representation of ethnic groups** amongst perpetrators from this published data set"; ethnicity "**is still not recorded for two-thirds of perpetrators**."
- **IICSA (2022):** in all six case-study areas, "**Poor or non-existent data collection makes it impossible to know whether any particular ethnic group is over-represented** as perpetrators of CSE by networks." (Per-area missing rates — Durham 35% of 1,900; Warwickshire >40% of 137; Tower Hamlets 86% of offenders in 147 reports — are reported *via IICSA's own published material*; the live IICSA HTML returned 403/405, and the headline conclusion is independently confirmed because it is quoted in Casey p.76.)

### Casey's net judgment — carry this verbatim posture
National data is too poor to conclude anything nationally; local data and the pattern of prosecutions "**at least warrant further examination**." Casey **explicitly rejects both** (i) using flawed national data to *dismiss* the over-representation claim, **and** (ii) asserting unevidenced certainty. As of 2025, recording offender ethnicity/nationality in CSA cases is being made a **statutory requirement** — an accepted Casey recommendation — precisely because the data was so incomplete. https://www.euronews.com/my-europe/2025/06/18/police-in-england-and-wales-to-collect-ethnicity-data-in-child-sexual-abuse-cases

### Drop-in takeaway for the page (keep the guardrail clause attached)
> "X% of UK CSE offenders are white British" and "~99% of Pakistan's child-abusers are Pakistani" are the **same statement wearing different clothes**: both are fixed by **who lives in the country**, not by how any group behaves. England & Wales is ~82% White (2021), so a white plurality of offenders is near-automatic; Pakistan's foreign-born population is only ~1.5% (UN/IOM 2019), so a Pakistani near-monopoly of offenders is literally automatic. Neither carries propensity information. Three categories must be held apart — **all CSA** (~500,000 children/year), **CSE**, and **group-based CSE** (~700 recorded offences, 2023) — and across the underlying data ethnicity is **routinely missing** (Home Office 2020; Casey 2025; IICSA §B.5). **The only sentence that means anything is a per-capita rate against the group's population base rate — and in the specific subset of group-based CSE, local force data (e.g. GMP: 52–54% Asian suspects vs 20.9% local population) shows Asian/Pakistani-heritage men over-represented even after base-rate correction. The aggregate finding and the subset finding are about two different phenomena, and both are true.**
