# Fault line 16 — Banking and finance (Islamic finance)

## Framing in Japanese context

Japanese-language Islamic-finance literature exists, mostly as policy and trade analysis rather than fault-line ethical treatment. The Financial Services Agency (FSA, 金融庁) opened a working group on Islamic finance after 2007; the 2008 amendment to the Banking Act (the *bunkō* / 兼業認可 rules) and subsequent JBIC sukuk arrangements gave Japanese institutions an Islamic-finance footprint without making it a domestic ethical debate. Most of the literature treats riba-prohibition as a foreign-jurisdiction parameter, not as a normative challenge to Japanese practice.

- **JBIC and Bank of Tokyo-Mitsubishi UFJ (now MUFG) sukuk arrangements**: documented in Nikkei and JCCME.
