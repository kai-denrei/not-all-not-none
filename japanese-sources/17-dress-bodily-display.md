# Fault line 17 — Dress and bodily display (hijab)

## Framing in Japanese context

Japan has no headscarf ban and no domestic headscarf debate. Hijab is permitted in Japanese schools, workplaces, and public service. The Japanese-language treatment is therefore largely comparative — coverage of French laïcité and the *loi 2004*, of the Quebec *Loi 21*, of the German *Lehrerinnen-Streit* — rather than a domestic policy issue. The 2022 Mahsa Amini moment shifted some Japanese coverage toward the *anti-compulsory*-hijab side, complementing the existing comparative work on *anti-banning*.

## Japan-specific cases

- **No headscarf-ban controversy in Japan**: hijab is worn in Japanese schools, public hospitals, and workplaces without legal challenge. This *non-event* is itself the Japanese-soil fact for the fault line.
