# Fault line 15 — Legal pluralism / sharia

## Framing in Japanese context

Japan has no *legal pluralism* problem on the European or British model — there is no Sharī'a council, no proposed parallel adjudication, no debate over Muslim arbitration. The Japanese-language treatment of legal pluralism therefore reads like comparative-law analysis aimed at understanding the British, Canadian, or Indonesian models, not domestic policy proposals.
