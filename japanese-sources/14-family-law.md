# Fault line 14 — Family law

## Framing in Japanese context

Japan's domestic family law (the *koseki* / family-register system, the Civil Code's marriage chapter) has no Sharī'a interface; foreign-national Muslims marrying or divorcing in Japan operate under Japanese private international law (Hōrei / 法の適用に関する通則法), which can incorporate the parties' national personal-status law. The Japanese-language scholarly treatment of Islamic family law is led by Mori Mariko at Kokushikan and by *Chūtō kenkyū* contributors.

## Japan-specific cases

- **Foreign-national Muslim divorces in Japan**: Japanese family courts have applied foreign personal-status law in divorce cases involving Muslim parties; Sakurada and other private-international-law commentary discusses the *ordre public* limits (e.g., a *ṭalāq* pronouncement registered in the parties' home country may or may not be recognized in Japan).
- **No Sharī'a-court parallel** exists in Japan; all family-law adjudication runs through the Japanese family-court (家庭裁判所) system applying Japanese choice-of-law rules.
