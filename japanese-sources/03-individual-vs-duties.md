# Fault line 03 — Individual rights vs. religious duties

## Framing in Japanese context

This is a Western-conceptual fault line that does not map cleanly onto Japanese moral discourse, where the individual–group axis is itself contested (uchi/soto, the *seken* concept). Japanese Islam-studies treats this fault line indirectly: through coverage of ex-Muslims, of the apostasy question (cross-cuts page 05), of women's-rights controversies, and through the Iranian morality-police coverage of 2022–2023 (the Mahsa Amini protests).

### 中東研究 (*Chūtō kenkyū*) — Mahsa Amini coverage
- Publisher: MEIJ.
- Date: late 2022 through 2023.
- URL: https://www.meij.or.jp/
- Summary: *Chūtō kenkyū* and JIME *Chūtō dōkō bunseki* both ran coverage of the 2022 Iranian protests sparked by the death of Mahsa Amini in morality-police custody. The Japanese press coverage (Asahi, Mainichi, NHK) was extensive.
- Relevance: The most recent high-profile case where the individual-rights-vs-religious-duties fault line was front-page news in Japan.

### 朝日新聞 (*Asahi Shimbun*) Mahsa Amini coverage 2022–2023
- Publisher: Asahi Shimbun.
- Date: September 2022 – through 2023.
- URL: https://www.asahi.com/
- Summary: Daily coverage of the Iranian protests, the morality police, and the executions of protest participants. Asahi maintained a Tehran correspondent's perspective.
- Relevance: Primary journalism.

## Japan-specific cases

- **Ex-Muslim coverage**: Japanese press coverage of European ex-Muslim controversies (Hirsi Ali, Charlie Hebdo) is the closest domestic engagement with this fault line. *Chūōkōron* and *Bungei shunjū* both ran feature essays.
