# Fault line 11 — Eschatology in public life

## Framing in Japanese context

Japanese Islam-studies treats Islamic eschatology theologically (Kosugi, Tonaga) but rarely as a *public-life* fault line. Japan has its own millenarian-movement memory (Aum Shinrikyō, 1995 sarin attacks; Sōka Gakkai's earlier internal debates) that tends to make Japanese readers cautious about apocalyptic political movements — but this caution does not generate a substantial Japanese-language literature on Islamic eschatology specifically.
