## Demographic and survey work

### 日本のムスリム人口推計 (*Nihon no Musurimu jinkō suikei* — Estimate of Muslim Population in Japan)
- Author/Publisher: Tanada Hirofumi (店田廣文), Waseda University, Faculty of Human Sciences. Initially published in *Waseda Asia Review* and the Waseda Institute for Asia-Pacific Studies working-paper series, with periodic updates.
- Date: First widely cited estimates from 2018; the 2019–2020 update is the standard Japanese-academic figure (~230,000, ~50% Japanese-national converts and Japanese-Muslim children).
- URL: <!-- TODO Phase 2: locate URL --> (Waseda WIAS repository)
- Summary: Tanada's series is the only systematic demographic estimate of Japan's Muslim population. Method: enumeration of mosques + nationality-of-residents data + birth-cohort estimates. The 2020 figure of ~230,000 (of whom ~50,000 Japanese nationals) is the figure cited by NHK, Asahi, METI, and the Japanese government.
- Relevance: Anchor reference for any page making a Japan-Islam claim (population, settlement geography, mosque count, halal demand).

### イスラーム教徒の社会的統合 (*Isurāmu kyōto no shakaiteki tōgō* — Social integration of Muslims)
- Author/Publisher: Tanada Hirofumi (店田廣文), Waseda University Press (早稲田大学出版部) and adjacent monograph chapters.
- Date: 2015, with subsequent edited-volume contributions.
- URL: <!-- TODO Phase 2: locate URL -->
- Summary: Book-length treatment of Muslim integration in Japan: mosque governance, halal infrastructure, intermarriage patterns, second-generation identity. The standard Japanese-language sociological treatment.
- Relevance: Cross-cuts pages 02, 05, 09, 10, 12, 13, 14, 17.

## Mainstream Japanese Islamic studies — overview works

### 現代イスラーム世界論 (*Gendai Isurāmu sekai-ron* — Theory of the contemporary Islamic world)
- Author/Publisher: Kosugi Yasushi (小杉泰), Nagoya University Press (名古屋大学出版会).
- Date: 2006.
- URL: <!-- TODO Phase 2: locate URL --> (Nagoya University Press catalog)
- Summary: Kosugi's standard university-level synthesis of Islamic political thought, the modern Islamic state debate, the umma concept, and Islamism. The most-cited Japanese textbook on Islamic political thought.
- Relevance: Pages 01, 02, 08, 10. Kosugi's treatment of the Caliphate question and political authority is the Japanese-academic baseline.

### イスラーム世界 (*Isurāmu sekai* — The Islamic world)
- Author/Publisher: Kosugi Yasushi (小杉泰), Iwanami Shoten (岩波書店), Iwanami Shinsho series.
- Date: 1994 (with later revised printings).

## Journals and periodicals

### 中東研究 (*Chūtō kenkyū* — Middle East Studies)
- Publisher: 中東調査会 (Middle East Institute of Japan, MEIJ) / formerly published also via JIME (日本エネルギー経済研究所中東研究センター, Japan Institute of Middle Eastern Economies).
- URL: https://www.meij.or.jp/ (MEIJ)
- Summary: The flagship Japanese-language journal of Middle East studies. Quarterly. Covers politics, economy, religion, security.
- Relevance: Cited across nearly every fault line where it has run a relevant article.

### 中東動向分析 (*Chūtō dōkō bunseki* — Middle East Trends Analysis)
- Publisher: 日本エネルギー経済研究所 中東研究センター (JIME, the Middle East Research Center of the Institute of Energy Economics, Japan).
- URL: https://www.jime.ieej.or.jp/
- Summary: JIME's monthly trend-analysis report. Tracks regional politics, Islamist movements, energy security, with implications for Japan.
- Relevance: Pages 01, 07, 10. The standard Japanese policy-analytical source.

### アジア経済 (*Ajia keizai* — Asian Economies)
- Publisher: 日本貿易振興機構アジア経済研究所 (IDE-JETRO).
- URL: https://www.ide.go.jp/
- Summary: IDE's flagship academic journal; runs Middle East and Islamic-economy articles (and a separate *Ajia keizai* monograph series).
- Relevance: Pages 16 (Islamic finance), 13 (halal markets).

### 中東協力センターニュース (*Chūtō kyōryoku sentā nyūsu* — JCCME News) and JCCME quarterly reports
- Publisher: 中東協力センター (Japan Cooperation Center for the Middle East, JCCME).
- URL: https://www.jccme.or.jp/
- Summary: Industry/government quarterly on Japan–Middle East cooperation. Heavy on energy, finance, trade.
- Relevance: Page 16. Less for theology-and-state pages.

### 中央公論 (*Chūōkōron*) and 文藝春秋 (*Bungei shunjū*)
- Publisher: Chūōkōron-Shinsha; Bungeishunjū.
- URL: https://chuokoron.jp/, https://bunshun.jp/
- Summary: General-interest opinion magazines. Both ran extensive coverage and commentary on the 2015 ISIS hostage crisis and on Japan's encounter with Islamist violence; both maintain occasional Middle East / Islam features.
- Relevance: Page 06, 07, 10.

### Foresight (新潮社)
- Publisher: Shinchōsha (新潮社).
- URL: https://www.fsight.jp/
- Summary: Subscription-based foreign-affairs journal; runs senior-correspondent dispatches on Middle East security and Islamist movements (Ikeuchi Satoshi 池内恵 has been a recurring contributor).
- Relevance: Pages 01, 06, 07, 10.

### Asahi Globe (朝日新聞GLOBE)
- Publisher: Asahi Shimbun (朝日新聞).
- URL: https://globe.asahi.com/
- Summary: Asahi's international long-form section. Has run features on European Muslim communities, halal food, and the post-Charlie-Hebdo debate, as well as on Japan's own Muslim communities.
- Relevance: Pages 06, 13, 17.

## Newspaper archives — anchor flashpoints

### 朝日新聞 (*Asahi Shimbun*) — Charlie Hebdo coverage and 2015 ISIS hostage coverage
- Publisher: Asahi Shimbun (朝日新聞).
- URL: https://www.asahi.com/ (with paywalled deep archive 朝日新聞デジタル / 聞蔵 Kikuzo II).
- Summary: Asahi ran daily coverage of the January 7, 2015 Charlie Hebdo attack and editorialized on free-expression-vs-religious-respect from a position more cautious than Le Monde's. On the ISIS hostage murders (January 20 / January 31 / February 1, 2015), Asahi was the lead Japanese print outlet.
- Relevance: Pages 06, 07.

- Publisher: Mainichi Shimbun (毎日新聞).

## Anchor flashpoint events — Japanese-language primary record

### The 2015 ISIS hostage murders (Yukawa Haruna 湯川遥菜 and Goto Kenji 後藤健二)
- January 20, 2015 — ISIS released the first hostage video demanding USD 200 million.
- January 24, 2015 — Yukawa reportedly killed (image released).
- January 31 / February 1, 2015 — Goto reportedly killed (video released).
- Coverage: Asahi, Mainichi, Yomiuri, Nikkei daily front pages; NHK live coverage; *Bungei shunjū* and *Chūōkōron* monthly features through 2015–2016; *Foresight* (Ikeuchi Satoshi) commentary.
- Relevance: Page 07 — the most-documented Japan-Islam-extremism flashpoint. Anchor for any Japanese discussion of jihadi violence.

### The 2002–2004 Iraq hostage crises
- Most-documented case: April 2004, three Japanese civilians (Imai Noriaki 今井紀明, Takato Nahoko 高遠菜穂子, Kōriyama Sōichirō 郡山総一郎) taken near Fallujah; released April 15, 2004. Followed by the October 2004 abduction and killing of Kōda Shōsei (香田証生).
- Coverage: Asahi, Mainichi, Yomiuri front pages April 2004 and October 2004; the resulting *jiko sekinin-ron* (自己責任論, "self-responsibility" debate) is itself a domestic landmark.
- Relevance: Page 07. Sets Japanese public memory of the Middle East before 2015.

### 2015 共謀罪 / 2017 conspiracy/anti-terror law
- Coverage: Asahi, Mainichi editorial-page coverage spring 2017; *Sekai* (世界, Iwanami) opposition essays.
- Relevance: Page 07 (counter-terror legislation and civil liberties).

### 1981 Iraqi nuclear-reactor strike (Osirak), Japanese reception
- Coverage: *Asahi shimbun* and *Yomiuri shimbun* June 1981 international pages; subsequent JIME / 中東研究 retrospectives.
- Relevance: Background context for Japanese reading on Israel–Arab security; minor for the fault lines, included for completeness as the request flagged it.

## Specialist scholars to cite by name across pages

- **Tanada Hirofumi (店田廣文)** — Waseda; Japanese-Muslim demographics and sociology.
- **Kosugi Yasushi (小杉泰)** — Kyoto University (emeritus, then Ritsumeikan); Islamic political thought.
- **Mori Mariko (森伸生)** — Kokushikan; Islamic law in modern application.
- **Ikeuchi Satoshi (池内恵)** — University of Tokyo; contemporary Islamism, jihadi movements, *Foresight* contributor; author of *Isurāmu-koku no shōgeki* (イスラーム国の衝撃, *The Shock of the Islamic State*, Bungei Shunjū, 2015).
- **Sakurai Keiko (桜井啓子)** — Waseda; Iran, Shi'a Islam, and Japanese Muslims.
- **Itagaki Yūzō (板垣雄三)** — University of Tokyo (emeritus); senior generation; Middle East historian whose framing shaped a generation of Japanese Islam-studies vocabulary.
- **Tonaga Yasushi (東長靖)** — Kyoto University; Sufism and Islamic mysticism.
- **Nakata Kō (中田考)** — Doshisha University (former); a controversial figure who acted as informal interlocutor during the 2015 ISIS hostage crisis; his publications matter for page 01 (caliphate) and 07.
- **Iizuka Masato (飯塚正人)** — Tokyo University of Foreign Studies (TUFS); Islamic intellectual history, contemporary Islamic thought.
