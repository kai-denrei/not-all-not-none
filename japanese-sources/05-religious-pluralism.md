# Fault line 05 — Religious pluralism / apostasy

## Framing in Japanese context

Japan's own religious-pluralism baseline is unusual: most Japanese self-report as not adhering to a single religion, while practicing Shintō and Buddhist rites situationally. This makes the Islamic-monotheist insistence on exclusive truth feel theologically distant. Japanese Islam-studies treats *dhimmī* / People of the Book questions historically (via Kosugi, Itagaki) and treats the apostasy question primarily through ISIS-era coverage (Yazidis, Christians of the Nineveh plain) and through the European ex-Muslim debate.
