# Fault line 10 — Nation-state vs. ummah

## Framing in Japanese context

Japan's own nation-state identity is one of the world's most internally coherent — a single language, an old polity, low immigration historically. The *umma* concept therefore registers in Japanese Islam-studies as genuinely *foreign* in a way that the European debate over post-national Muslim identity does not. Japanese-language treatment leans on Kosugi Yasushi's framework of the *umma* as both pre-modern and currently active across modern nation-state boundaries.

## Japan-specific cases

- **Tokyo Camii** (東京ジャーミイ, Yoyogi-Uehara), Japan's largest mosque, is governed by the Turkish Diyanet — a concrete instance of the nation-vs-ummah question on Japanese soil (Turkish-state religious authority operating a mosque inside Japan).
- **The 2015 hostage crisis as nation-state-vs-ummah moment**: Goto Kenji and Yukawa Haruna were held by a polity (ISIS) that explicitly rejected the modern nation-state and the Japanese passport.
