# Fault line 13 — Dietary law and shared life (halal)

## Framing in Japanese context

Halal infrastructure in Japan grew out of the post-2010 inbound-tourism push (METI, JNTO, JTB initiatives targeting Indonesian and Malaysian visitors), the run-up to the 2019 Rugby World Cup and the 2020 (held 2021) Tokyo Olympics, and Tanada-documented growth of the resident Muslim community. Japan does *not* have a state halal authority; multiple competing private certifiers operate (Japan Halal Association, Muslim Professional Japan Association MPJA, Nippon Asia Halal Association NAHA, etc.). Trade-press coverage is dense; school-canteen accommodation is episodic and locally varied.

## Japan-specific cases

- **2019 Rugby World Cup and Tokyo 2020 Olympics halal infrastructure**: government push for halal-friendly certification at venues, hotels, and Narita/Haneda; documented in JNTO and JTA reports.
- **Shin-Ōkubo and Asakusa halal-restaurant clusters**: the densest halal-restaurant geographies in Tokyo, documented in *Asahi Globe* and Nikkei features.
- **School-canteen accommodation**: varies by municipality; the most-cited progressive example is Tochigi-prefecture cases, plus selected Saitama and Tokyo schools.
- **The halal-certifier fragmentation**: a Japan-specific story — multiple private bodies, no JAKIM-equivalent state authority. Documented in METI and JCCME reports.
