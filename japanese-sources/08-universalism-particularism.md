# Fault line 08 — Universalism vs. particularism

## Framing in Japanese context

The Japanese intellectual tradition has its own universalism-vs-particularism debate (the *kindaika-ron*, *Nihon-jinron*, the post-1945 universalism of Maruyama Masao). Japanese Islam-studies tends to read Islamic universalism comparatively against this domestic axis: Kosugi Yasushi's *Gendai Isurāmu sekai-ron* presents the *umma* as a universalist project comparable in ambition to (and in some respects more successful than) Western liberal universalism.
