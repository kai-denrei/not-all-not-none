# Fault line 09 — Education

## Framing in Japanese context

Japan does not have an Islamic-school controversy on the European model — there are very few Islamic schools in Japan (the International Islamia School Otsuka in Tokyo and a small number of supplementary madrasas attached to mosques). The Japanese debate, where it exists, concerns school-canteen halal accommodation (cross-cuts page 13), Ramadan accommodation, and the question of whether Japanese-Muslim children are integrating into the standard 小学校/中学校 system or being privately schooled. Tanada Hirofumi's work tracks this.

## Japan-specific cases

- **International Islamia School Otsuka** (大塚): the most-cited Japanese Islamic school. Tanada documents.
- **School-canteen halal accommodation cases**: Japanese school-board responses vary by municipality. Episodic press coverage (Asahi, Mainichi) of individual schools providing halal-compatible meals or having Muslim parents opt into bringing bento.
- **Swimming-class and PE accommodation**: a small NHK / Asahi feature literature on individual cases of Muslim girls in Japanese public schools.
