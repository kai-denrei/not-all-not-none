# Fault line 18 — Calendar and time

## Framing in Japanese context

Japan's calendar landscape is itself plural — Gregorian dates plus 元号 (*nengō*, era names: 平成, 令和) for official use. The Hijri calendar is treated as anthropological curiosity. There is essentially no Japanese-language scholarly treatment of the Hijri calendar as a public-life fault line; the topic appears in introductory Islam textbooks and in JIME / *Chūtō kenkyū* coverage of Saudi-state practice (Mecca-time vs UTC, Ramadan-start moonsighting controversies).
